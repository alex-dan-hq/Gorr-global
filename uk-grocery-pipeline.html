UK Grocery Retail — Leadership Pipeline

Live restructuring 2025–26

Five levels of leadership in UK grocery retail

Every role across all 10 major UK grocers maps to one of five Pipeline levels. Level 2 is the largest management population and the critical failure point — the transition from individual contributor to people manager.

Active restructuring across the sector

Tesco: three restructures since 2021 — 1,750 manager roles cut in 2023, Deputy SM eliminated, Team Support removed in Extras. Sainsbury's: "Next Level" strategy, 3,000 roles cut, 20% senior management reduction. Asda: July 2025 merger of Section Manager + Trading Manager into single "Manager" role. Co-op: four tiers collapsed to three, 2,000+ roles impacted. Waitrose: "Simpler Shops" cut 678 roles, removed departmental silos.

L5 Manage Business 1 per company
CEO Managing Director

Total business profitability across all functions, formats, and geographies.

Current holders

Ken Murphy (Tesco Group), Simon Roberts (Sainsbury's), Allan Leighton (Asda, Exec Chairman), Rami Baitiéh (Morrisons), Giles Hurley (Aldi UK/IE), Ryan McDonnell (Lidl GB), Shirine Khoury-Haq (Co-op Group), Nish Kankiwala (JLP/Waitrose), Stuart Machin (M&S), Tarsem Dhaliwal (Iceland).

Primary challenge

Balancing short-term financial performance with long-term investment in people and technology. Managing the simultaneous pressures of NLW inflation, discounter competition, and online fulfilment costs.

L4 Manage Function 8–12 per company
Commercial Director Supply Chain Director People Director CTO / CIO CFO Retail Ops Director Regional Managing Director

Board/ExCo members owning entire functional domains. Setting functional strategy and owning the P&L. Includes Regional Managing Directors at Aldi (13 regions) and Lidl (13 regions).

Purpose

Translate business strategy into functional execution. Own the talent pipeline, strategic roadmap, and budget for their domain. At Sainsbury's, Tracey Clements holds the combined Chief Retail, Logistics & Supply Chain Officer role from September 2025.

Primary challenge

Moving from operational excellence to strategic thinking. The urge to micro-manage rather than set direction. Lidl restructured from 3 COOs to 2 in March 2025 — even at board level, layers are being compressed.

L3 Manage Managers 200–600 per retailer
Regional Manager Area Manager District Manager Store Manager (large format) Store Operations Director

Develop and manage people who themselves manage others. Area Manager spans vary widely: Aldi 3–5 stores (~215–360 AMs), Sainsbury's ~25 stores (~56 AMs), Tesco 12–25 stores (~150–300 AMs), Co-op ~20 stores.

Key insight

Asda axed 13 regional managers in January 2025 — exact replacement structure unconfirmed. Sainsbury's created 4 new Regional Store Directors specifically for convenience stores (separating convenience from supermarket leadership). Aldi Regional Manager salary: £57,287/yr avg.

Primary challenge

The temptation to "fix" a struggling store directly — bypassing the Store Manager. The classic Level 3 error. Large-format Store Managers sit here: they manage Department/Team Managers who in turn manage frontline staff.

L2 Manage OthersTarget layer 8,000–18,000 per retailer
Team Manager (Tesco) CTM (Sainsbury's) Manager (Asda) Dept Manager (Morrisons) Section Manager (M&S/Waitrose) Store Manager (discount/conv.) ASM (Aldi)

The largest management population. Directly manage 5–20+ frontline staff. The most common Pipeline failure point — the transition from "best Customer Assistant" to "manager of Customer Assistants." Currently being radically reshaped: broader spans, fewer roles, higher pay expectations.

What's changing

Titles are converging: Tesco's "Team Manager" (formerly Dept Manager), Sainsbury's "CTM" (replaced 3 old roles), Asda's new unified "Manager" (merged from Section Manager + Trading Manager). Spans of control are widening as layers collapse. Only Tesco has created a dedicated Dotcom management chain at this level.

Primary challenge

Letting go of frontline work they were promoted for. The identity shift from individual contributor to people manager. Compounded by pay compression: the gap between CA pay (£12–13.50/hr) and L2 management pay (£13–15/hr) is now as little as £1/hr at some retailers.

L1→2 Transition Zone Declining — being restructured
Shift Leader (Tesco) Team Leader (Asda/Co-op) Retail Shift Manager (Lidl) Deputy SM (Aldi/Lidl) Supervisor (Iceland)

Semi-management: run shifts and coordinate work, hourly-paid, without full people-management responsibility. Tesco is replacing Team Leaders with Shift Leaders (£14.76/hr). Lidl's Retail Shift Manager (~£25,365/yr) is a tier that Aldi doesn't have.

Status

Co-op eliminated its Team Manager layer entirely (4→3 tiers, 2,000+ jobs). Sainsbury's axed Team Leader, Store Supervisor, and Department Manager roles in 2017. At Tesco, Shift Leaders are the new standard — Team Leaders/Team Support are being phased out.

Implication

As this layer disappears at legacy grocers, the L1→L2 jump becomes steeper — no intermediate step. The discounters never had this layer (Aldi has zero supervisory tier; Lidl's Retail Shift Manager is the sole exception).

L1 Manage Self 80,000–300,000 per retailer
Customer Assistant Online Picker / Dotcom Personal Shopper Checkout Operator Store Colleague Butcher / Baker / Fishmonger (Morrisons) Stock Assistant (Aldi) Delivery Driver

The bulk of the workforce. Pay ranges from NLW (£12.21 Morrisons) to £13.50/hr (Aldi). Aldi's paid breaks add ~£0.75/hr effective premium. No retailer maintains dedicated compliance roles at store level — food safety is embedded in management remits.

Specialisation

Morrisons is unique with skilled craft roles: Butcher, Baker, Fishmonger on Market Street — requiring trade skills, not just retail competence. Aldi distinguishes Store Assistants (£13.50/hr, general) from Stock Assistants (£12.97/hr, inventory-focused). Most retailers have converged on multi-skilled generalist CAs.

Career timeline

Typical internal progression without graduate scheme: CA (0–2 yrs) → Team Leader/Shift Leader (1–3 yrs) → Dept Manager (3–5 yrs) → Deputy SM (5–8 yrs) → Store Manager (7–12 yrs) → Area Manager (10–15+ yrs). Aldi's graduate scheme compresses this to Area Manager in 12 months.

The restructuring wave — five retailers, one direction

Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, Co-op and Waitrose have all executed significant delayering between 2023 and 2026. They are converging toward the flat model Aldi has operated since entering the UK in 1990.

Why this is happening

Pay compression

NLW rises to £12.71/hr (Apr 2026). First-line management pays £13–15/hr. The premium for management responsibility is now as little as £1/hr. Sainsbury's base rate rose 42% in five years. Asda's £12.60/hr and Morrisons' £12.21/hr both need upward adjustment to stay legal.

NIC + compliance costs

April 2025 NICs increase alone cost Sainsbury's £140M. Employment Rights Act 2025 adds SSP from day one, scheduling compliance, rights exposure. Fewer management positions = lower cost base. Intensifying discounter price competition compresses margins further.

Online + operational complexity

Only Tesco created dedicated Dotcom management. Others absorbed online into existing roles — invisible span-of-control expansion. Night shift management is a dedicated role at Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, and M&S (15–40+ colleagues per store, premiums £1.20–2.35/hr).

Union recognition

USDAW recognised: Tesco, Sainsbury's, Morrisons, Co-op, Asda (~360–370k members). Partnership model — negotiates annual pay deals, formal consultation during restructures. Won Supreme Court case vs Tesco (2024) on "fire and rehire."

Not USDAW: Aldi, Lidl (direct employer-employee, German model), M&S (elected BIG representatives), Waitrose (Partnership democratic governance), Iceland.

Head office functions

Nine support functions sit behind the store estate. Commercial/Buying is the largest. People/HR is where the Pipeline product buyer sits.

Commercial / Buying / Trading

Commercial Director / Trading Director

The largest HQ function. Product range, pricing, supplier relationships, promotions, margin. Split into Buying, Category Management, and Trading.

Supply Chain / Logistics

Supply Chain Director

Supplier to shelf. Distribution centres, transport, replenishment, demand forecasting. Morrisons is unique: UK's 2nd-largest fresh food manufacturer (abattoirs, packing houses, fish processing).

People / HR

People Director / Chief People Officer

100k–340k employees per retailer. Talent, L&D, employee relations (USDAW for 5 of 10 retailers), comp & benefits, workforce planning. The Pipeline product buyer sits here.

Technology / IT

CTO / CIO

Store systems (POS, self-checkout, ESL), online platforms, supply chain systems, data & analytics. Asda deployed "Store Assist" picking tech across 534 stores, training 26,000 pickers.

Finance

Group Finance Director / CFO

Financial planning, reporting, audit, tax, treasury, investor relations. Private equity ownership at Morrisons (CD&R) and Asda (TDR Capital 67.5%) changes dynamics.

Marketing / Customer

Customer Director / CMO

Brand, advertising, customer insight. Loyalty: Clubcard (Tesco), Nectar (Sainsbury's). Retail media networks growing. Lidl created a new Chief Customer Officer role (March 2025).

Property / Real Estate

Property Director

Physical estate: lease negotiations, new store development, format strategy. Aldi targeting 1,500 UK stores (from 1,080). M&S converting former Homebase sites to 18–22k sq ft Food Halls.

Online / Digital

CDO / Head of Online

Online platform, click-and-collect, rapid delivery. Only Tesco has dedicated store-level Dotcom management. Others use partnerships (Deliveroo, Just Eat) or existing staff. Aldi/Lidl don't sell groceries online.

Legal / Compliance

General Counsel

Food safety, competition law, employment law, government relations. Employment Rights Act 2025 and NIC increases are driving structural decisions across the sector.
